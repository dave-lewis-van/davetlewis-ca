## Context

Alida is an enterprise customer experience platform used by research analysts and administrators. The Alida Help Center serves both audiences — analysts running surveys and interpreting results, and admins managing users, permissions, and platform configuration.

## Challenge

The Alida platform was in active development, with features shipping in regular agile cycles. Documentation had to keep pace with releases while covering a broad set of features and teams. The documentation covered a full-featured research and analytics platform, enterprise administration features, and community engagement tools.

## Approach

As a member of the documentation team, I partnered with Product Management, Engineering, QA, and UX to stay ahead of the release schedule. I built up familiarity with the platform features as a user before writing, then structured documentation around the workflows each audience actually performs.

Throughout my time at Alida, I was focused on the reporting and analytics areas of the platform, and on Adminsistator features such as [Single Sign-on (SSO)](https://alidasupport.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/45348005094676). More recently, I focused on documentation for the platform's first generative AI feature, the [AI assistant for reporting](https://alidasupport.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/46395262958100). I also wrote documentation for complex analysis and reporting features including [weighting](https://alidasupport.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/45343934676884), [significance testing](https://alidasupport.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/45343868627220), and AI-powered [sentiment analysis](https://alidasupport.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/45343794894228).

## Outcome

Along with the other members of the documentation team, I maintained current and accurate documentation across the Alida Platform, shipping documentation alongside every release.
